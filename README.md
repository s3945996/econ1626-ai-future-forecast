# econ1626-ai-future-forecast

## Project title

**Australia’s AI economy, 2027–2030: fast diffusion, slow institutions**

## Project overview

This repository contains an interactive web essay for ECON1626 Assessment 3.

The project forecasts how artificial intelligence may reshape the Australian economy between 2027 and 2030. It is inspired by the AI 2027 scenario method, but focuses on Australia’s national economy rather than a global superintelligence race.

The central thesis is that AI will create a productivity shock for Australia, but not automatically a broad prosperity shock. The final outcome will depend on complementary investment in skills, trusted data systems, cyber safety, competition policy, public-sector adoption and energy-aware compute infrastructure.

## Research question

How will generative and agentic AI reshape the Australian economy by 2027–2030, and what policy choices could shift Australia toward a more productive, inclusive and trustworthy AI transition?

## Thesis

By 2030, generative and agentic AI will be widely embedded in Australian workplaces. However, the benefits will be uneven unless Australia invests in the institutions that allow AI capability to become shared productivity: skills, governance, competition, public-sector capability and trusted adoption by small and medium-sized enterprises.
